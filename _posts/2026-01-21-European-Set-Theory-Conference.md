The 10th European Set Theory Conference continues a series that began in 2007.
Since its first edition, the European Set Theory Conference has been a key venue
for advancing research in the field featuring renowned speakers and fostering
discussion and collaboration. The meeting will be held in Helsinki in May 18-22,
2026.

### Programme

There will be plenary talks by

- Tom Benhamou
- Piotr Borodulin-Nadzieja
- Jonathan Cancino Manriquez
- William Chan
- Monroe Eskew
- Jan Grebik
- Siiri Kivimäki
- Alexandra Kwiatkowska
- Farmer Schlutzenberg
- Benjamin Siskind
- Andrea Vaccaro
- Jenna Zomback

and tutorials by

- Vera Fischer
- Menachem Magidor

There will also be sessions for contributed talks – see Abstract
submission below.


### Abstract submission

The conference will include sessions for contributed talks. The talks
will be 20 min long. If you are interested in contributing a talk,
please send a title and a max 2 page abstract to estc2026@helsinki.fi
with the subject "Abstract", by the deadline of March 1, 2026.


### Important dates

February 18: Deadline for travel support applications  
March 1: Deadline for contributed talk abstracts  
March: Registration opens  
March 13: Notification of acceptance of contributed talks  
April 1: Deadline for early registration  
May 18-22: ESTC2026


### Travel support (deadline February 18)

#### ASL student travel awards
Student members of the Association for Symbolic Logic (ASL) may apply
for travel support at ASL. Note that such applications have to be
submitted at least 3 months prior to the meeting. For precise
instructions on how to apply for ASL support, please see the ASL website
https://aslonline.org/student-travel-awards/

#### Local travel support
In addition to the ASL travel awards, there is also some local funding
available as travel support. To apply, send a mail to
estc2026@helsinki.fi with the subject "Travel support" containing a
motivation letter and an estimation of the support needed.
